Disclaimer

Short version: everything here is a student learning how to build AI. None of it is meant to diagnose you, give you advice, or replace a real professional.

It's a learning project

RLcapstone.ai is where I keep my personal AI projects. I built the models, apps, and results here to learn how this stuff works from start to finish. Nothing here has been checked by doctors, audited by anyone, or approved by any official group, and none of it is a real product or service.

Not medical advice

A couple of these projects use health data (like MoleCheck). They're just demos of image AI, nothing more. They are not medical devices, they can't diagnose anything, and you should never use what they say to make a health decision.

If you're worried about a mole, a skin change, or anything about your health, see a real doctor. A model saying "looks fine" is not a green light, and it saying "flagged" is not a diagnosis — only an actual doctor can tell you what's really going on.

The scores aren't the full story

The numbers I report (accuracy, catch rate, and so on) are from specific practice datasets under specific conditions. In the real world — different cameras, lighting, skin tones, or situations — a model can do a lot worse. AI fails in weird ways, and I point those failures out on purpose, because understanding where things break is a big part of the point.

No guarantees

Everything here — the writing, the code, the models — is provided "as is," with no promises. If you use any of it, that's on you, and I'm not responsible for anything that happens because of it.

Your data stays with you

These projects try to run the AI right on your own device. When a demo or app looks at an image, that happens locally — nothing gets uploaded to a server. The datasets I trained on are free public research datasets (like the ISIC Archive), used under their licenses.

MoleCheck live demo

The same AI from the MoleCheck app, running right in your browser. Your photo is checked on your device — it's never uploaded, and this site has no server to send it to anyway.

Before you try it — this is not a real medical test.
  • It's a school project that shows off image AI. It can't diagnose skin cancer or anything else.
  • A "lower-risk" result is not a green light. It misses about 1 in 10 dangerous moles even on its test data, and real phone photos are harder than the medical ones it learned on.
  • If a mole is new, changing, bleeding, or worrying you at all, see a skin doctor — no matter what this page says.

Whatever the score says: this can't see what a real doctor sees. If you're worried about this mole, get it looked at in person.

What happens to my photo?

Nothing leaves your browser. The AI (a 21 MB file) downloads to your device once, and all the checking happens locally. You can even turn off your internet after the page loads and it'll still work.

Tips for a decent result

  • One mole per photo, centered and in focus, filling most of the frame.
  • Good, even lighting — no shadows across the mole.
  • It learned on special medical close-ups, so regular phone photos are outside its comfort zone — treat the score as a rough hint only.

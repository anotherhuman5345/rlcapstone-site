Blood-cell AI — live demo

Pick a real blood-cell photo below. The AI sorts it into one of four types — a healthy look-alike or one of three cancer stages — right in your browser. Nothing is uploaded.

Before you try it — this can't diagnose leukemia.
  • Real leukemia diagnosis takes doctors and lab tests like bone-marrow analysis — never a single cell photo.
  • This is a demo of image AI on a research dataset, and (as the write-up explains) its big score is inflated by how the data is split. Treat every answer as a learning example.
  • If you have any health worry, see a real doctor.

Pick a cell

Each thumbnail is a real photo of one white blood cell from the test set (photos the AI didn't train on).

On this dataset the AI looks almost perfect — which is exactly the thing to be suspicious of. The write-up explains why, and what the fixed v2 will show.

What am I looking at?

Each photo is a single white blood cell, stained and taken under a microscope. Three of the types (Early Pre-B, Pre-B, Pro-B) are stages of the same blood cancer; the fourth is a healthy look-alike. Telling them apart is hard even for experts — which is what makes it a cool AI problem, and why testing it honestly matters so much.

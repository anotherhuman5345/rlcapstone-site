Brain-wave AI — live demo

Pick a real 19-sensor brain-wave recording below — each is a 2-second snippet from a kid the AI never saw while training. It sorts the recording right in your browser; nothing is uploaded.

A demo for learning — it can't diagnose ADHD. ADHD is diagnosed by doctors, never from a quick brain scan. This just guesses whether a research recording came from the study's ADHD or non-ADHD group — pattern-matching, not a medical test. If you're worried about attention or focus, talk to a professional.

Pick a recording

Each is labeled with the group the kid was in during the study: Control (no ADHD) or ADHD. Tap one to see its 19-sensor brain waves and the AI's guess.

This AI tells apart the study's two groups with about 92% accuracy on kids it never saw — genuinely cool, but it's a small study and it's telling apart groups, not diagnosing anyone. See the write-up for the honest numbers.

What am I looking at?

Each row is one sensor on the scalp (Fp1, Fp2, F3, and so on), and each squiggle is 2 seconds of brain activity. The AI reads all 19 sensors at once. It only ever learned from other kids, so a correct guess here means the pattern it found carries over to people it's never seen.

ADHD EEG live demo

Pick a real 19-channel EEG recording below — each is a 2-second window from a child the model never saw during training. The model classifies it in your browser; nothing is uploaded.

Educational demo — this cannot diagnose ADHD. ADHD is diagnosed clinically by professionals, never from a brief EEG. This classifies whether a research recording came from the study's ADHD or control group — a pattern-matching exercise, not a medical test. If you have concerns about attention or focus, speak with a qualified clinician.

Pick a recording

Each is labelled by the group the child belonged to in the study: Control or ADHD. Tap one to see its 19-channel EEG and the model's call.

This model separates the study's two groups with about 92% accuracy on unseen subjects — genuinely interesting, but on a small cohort and for group membership, not diagnosis. See the write-up for the honest numbers and caveats.

What am I looking at?

Each row is one electrode on the scalp (Fp1, Fp2, F3 … following the international 10–20 layout), and each trace is 2 seconds of brain electrical activity sampled 128 times per second. The model reads all 19 channels together. It was trained only on recordings from other children, so a correct call here means the pattern it learned transfers to people it has never encountered.

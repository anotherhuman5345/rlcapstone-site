A demo for learning — not a medical tool. This sorts pre-recorded research heartbeats to show how an AI reads an ECG. It can't read a live ECG and it's not a substitute for a doctor or a real heart monitor. If you have symptoms or a heart-rhythm worry, get medical care.

Each beat is labeled with its real type (from cardiologists): N normal, S supraventricular, V ventricular, F fusion. Watch what the AI gets right — and where it slips up.

A demo on hand-picked research data makes any AI look better than it is. On patients it's truly never seen, this one catches ventricular beats well but misses most supraventricular ones — see the write-up for the honest numbers and why.

What am I looking at?

Each squiggle is one heartbeat — about 0.7 seconds of the ECG, centered on the beat's sharp spike. The AI only ever learned the shape of these. That's why it reads ventricular beats (which look clearly different) but struggles with supraventricular ones, which look almost normal — their real giveaway is timing, which the AI can't see. Fixing that is exactly what the next version is about.

Guessing a stock's risk — and testing if news helps

An AI that guesses whether a stock's coming week will be Low, Medium, or High risk (how much it bounces around) from its price history and the news about it. I mostly built it to answer one question honestly: does the news part actually help?

Not financial advice. This is a school AI project. It guesses how bumpy a stock is, not whether it goes up or down, and nothing here tells you to buy or sell anything. Here's the full disclaimer.

Why bumpiness, not direction

Guessing whether a stock goes up or down tomorrow is basically a coin flip. But guessing how much it'll move is a different story, because calm and crazy stretches come in streaks — calm weeks tend to follow calm weeks, wild weeks follow wild ones. That streakiness is a real pattern the model can learn, which is why it hits 56% out of three choices where random guessing would only get 33%.

Watching it learn

The model trains in seconds and settles around 64% on the practice weeks, then drops to 56% on the later weeks it had never seen. That drop is normal and honest — the market moved into a stretch the model hadn't practiced on.

The honest headline: the news didn't help

The whole point of this project was the "news" part, so the result that matters is the comparison. When I trained the exact same model on price clues only — no news — it scored 57.2%, actually a tiny bit better than the 56.3% with news included. The news added nothing; if anything, a little noise.

That's not a bug and it's not a letdown — it's the most useful thing I found. A once-a-day average of news mood is a pretty weak, blurry signal, and once you already have the "how bumpy has it been lately" clues, most of what you can predict about next week is already covered. Reporting that honestly — instead of quietly deleting the test and claiming "news helps!" — is the difference between a demo and a real experiment.

Where it struggles

It messes up exactly where you'd expect: it reads calm weeks (76% right) and wild weeks (60%) pretty well, but the Medium middle is hard (27%), because "medium" bumpiness is basically a blurry line between the other two. That's an honest, understandable weak spot, not a mystery.

What's next (v2)

If the news is ever going to earn its spot, it needs to be smarter than a daily average: actually reading each headline instead of averaging a mood score, using more years of data across different kinds of markets, and testing whether news helps more for guessing direction than bumpiness. Those results will show up in the table above — including, honestly, if the news still doesn't help.

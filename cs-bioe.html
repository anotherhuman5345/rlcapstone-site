Computer Science + Bioengineering

This portfolio sits at the intersection of computer science and bioengineering. Each project pairs a concrete computational method — deep learning and biomedical signal or image processing — with a health-technology problem, and each is carried through the full lifecycle: dataset, model, rigorous evaluation, and a working application. The sections below map that work to the two disciplines.

Why I'm pursuing this major

I am pursuing a degree in Computer Science + Bioengineering because it represents the ideal integration of my interests in computational problem-solving and the advancement of human health through technology. This interdisciplinary field allows me to combine rigorous training in computer science with bioengineering principles to develop innovative solutions for complex biomedical challenges.

My independent projects have given me practical experience at this intersection. For instance, I developed MoleCheck, an on-device skin lesion risk-awareness application using YOLO11 for computer vision, and an ECG Arrhythmia classifier based on 1D convolutional neural networks. These projects required me to navigate the full development lifecycle — from dataset curation and model training to deployment and critical evaluation of real-world limitations, such as generalization gaps in patient data. They reinforced my understanding of the technical and ethical considerations involved in applying AI to medical domains, including data privacy and model robustness.

A CS + Bioengineering program will equip me with advanced knowledge in areas such as biomedical data analysis, computational modeling of biological systems, medical imaging, and healthcare informatics. I am particularly eager to engage in research and collaborative projects that translate computational techniques into meaningful clinical impact. Ultimately, I aim to contribute to the development of responsible, effective technologies that improve diagnostics, personalized medicine, and patient outcomes.

This major aligns perfectly with my long-term goal of working at the forefront of computational health technologies, and I am excited to bring my technical foundation and dedication to an institution that values interdisciplinary innovation.

How the portfolio maps to the two disciplines

The computer science throughline is applied machine learning done carefully: convolutional neural networks, leakage-aware evaluation, and the engineering needed to ship a model to a phone or a browser. The bioengineering throughline is the domain grounding: physiological signals (ECG, EEG), medical imaging (dermatology, hematology), and a clinical evaluation mindset that favors sensitivity, specificity, and ROC-AUC over raw accuracy. The recurring theme across both is responsible, honest health technology — privacy-preserving inference, clearly stated limitations, and explicitly non-diagnostic framing.
